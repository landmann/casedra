# Casablanca — Execution Plan For Q2 To Q4 2026

**Date:** 2026-04-18  
**Window covered:** 2026-04-17 to 2026-12-31  
**Purpose:** turn the 36-month strategy into a concrete operating plan for the rest of 2026

---

## 1. What 2026 Must Achieve

By the end of 2026, Casablanca must no longer look like:

- a promising prototype
- an AI media demo
- a founder intuition project

By the end of 2026, Casablanca must look like:

- a live workflow product
- a repeatable Madrid motion
- a company with clear proof that agencies will pay for response and seller-performance improvement

The year-end objective is to enter January 2027 in position to hit:

- `200` active paying agencies by `2027-01-31`
- a trusted inbox workflow
- a seller-acquisition beta that is commercially credible

---

## 2. Year-End Outcomes

### Minimum acceptable by 2026-12-31

- `140+` active paying agencies
- `>=80%` live-channel connection rate in paying accounts
- `>=75%` inbox weekly active rate
- deploy time from signed to live `<=7 days`
- clear case-study base from Madrid
- first seller-acquisition workflows in beta
- evaluation coverage on core AI workflows
- weekly proof memo ritual operating reliably

### Strong year-end outcome

- `160-180` active paying agencies
- stable onboarding system
- first Barcelona motion ready to scale
- first internal benchmark reporting across the customer base
- reusable playbook library from top-performing accounts

## 4. Q2 2026 — Prove The Wedge

**Window:** 2026-04-17 to 2026-06-30

### Primary objective

Prove agencies will trust Casablanca with live demand and pay for it.

### Commercial targets

- 10 design partners live by `2026-05-31`
- 8 paying agencies by `2026-05-31`
- 15 active paying agencies by `2026-06-30`

### Product targets

- WhatsApp and forwarded lead workflows live
- manual takeover reliable
- inbox usable daily by design partners
- first reporting template delivered
- confidence thresholds and escalation rules live
- evaluation harness v1 for core flows

### Team targets

- founders own all sales
- realtor team runs audits and onboarding support
- developer team focuses only on connection, inbox, handoff, and reporting

### Founder responsibilities

#### Founder 1 — Revenue

- recruit design partners
- run office demos
- close first paying agencies
- document objections daily

#### Founder 2 — Product / Engineering

- keep scope narrow
- ship connection, inbox, and handoff reliability
- instrument every key event
- build the first trustworthy evaluation loop

#### Founder 3 — Operations / Finance

- define onboarding checklist
- build customer health tracking
- run weekly account reviews

### Realtor / operator responsibilities

- perform workflow audits
- help connect channels
- collect baseline response behavior
- turn first wins into usable proof

### Developer responsibilities

- connection flow
- parsing
- state machine
- takeover
- reporting v1
- evaluation coverage for core scenarios

### Q2 kill criteria

If by `2026-06-30` fewer than 70% of paying agencies have live channels connected and weekly usage, the wedge is weaker than expected and the roadmap must narrow again.

## 5. Q3 2026 — Prove Madrid Repeatability

**Window:** 2026-07-01 to 2026-09-30

### Primary objective

Turn founder-led wins into a repeatable local machine.

### Commercial targets

- hit `25` active paying agencies by `2026-07-31`
- reach `50-60` active paying agencies by `2026-09-30`
- maintain churn below plan while growing

### Product targets

- stronger routing and assignment
- manager visibility
- onboarding friction reduced materially
- reporting good enough to drive renewals and referrals
- weekly manager proof memo live
- first reusable response playbooks captured from healthy accounts

### Org targets

- first deployment-heavy operator hired
- first customer success / onboarding capacity added
- founder time still field-heavy, but onboarding is less founder-dependent

### Founder responsibilities

#### Founder 1

- move from one-off closes to repeatable pitch
- build referral loop from healthy accounts
- formalize pricing and packaging discipline

#### Founder 2

- improve setup reliability
- reduce deployment time
- improve reporting quality
- turn field behavior into product defaults where it repeats

#### Founder 3

- define account health program
- create churn prevention playbook
- maintain cash discipline

### Realtor / operator responsibilities

- own onboarding
- run weekly ROI reviews with customers
- collect case studies
- codify objections and rescue patterns

### Developer responsibilities

- routing logic
- manager views
- deployment diagnostics
- weekly metrics surfaces
- playbook and evaluation tooling

### Internal checkpoint by 2026-09-30

By the end of Q3 we should know:

- whether Madrid is genuinely repeatable
- whether onboarding can scale
- whether the product is sticky enough to justify the next expansion step

If the answer is no, we should delay Barcelona scale and keep tightening Madrid.

### Founder responsibilities

- push high-quality account acquisition, not vanity growth
- turn seller-performance proof into upsell narrative

- ship seller-acquisition beta carefully
- keep inbox reliability high while adding module depth
- prevent CRM sprawl
- keep trust and evaluation quality above growth pressure

#### Founder 3

- ensure support and success systems keep churn low
- prepare fundraising materials off real metrics, not optimism
- maintain weekly health reviews

### Realtor / operator responsibilities

- seller-workflow discovery
- beta onboarding for seller suite
- stronger ROI proof collection
- city expansion field prep

### Developer responsibilities

- seller lead capture flows
- owner nurture workflows
- stronger reporting and benchmark views
- migration quality improvements

The answer must come from usage, retention, and deployment speed, not ambition alone.

## 9. Hiring Plan Through 2026

### Hire only if Q2 and Q3 metrics justify it

1. deployment-heavy operator
2. customer success / onboarding lead
3. additional full-stack engineer focused on workflow reliability

### Do not hire in 2026 unless forced

- broad content marketing team
- large brand function
- heavy enterprise sales layer
- operations managers without direct ownership

The company should still feel founder-led at the end of 2026.

---

## 10. What Gets Cut Automatically

These items should be cut on sight if they threaten 2026 focus:

- broad CRM parity work
- consumer portal discussion
- complex marketplace programs
- deep mobile app ambitions
- speculative country work before Madrid repeatability
- media features that do not improve sales or seller presentations

---

## 11. Non-Negotiable Rules For 2026

1. Every signed account should get to live quickly.
2. Every live account should receive a weekly proof report.
3. Every product release must help activation, usage, retention, or seller-suite readiness.
4. Every founder must spend time in the field weekly.
5. Every month must end with an honest gate review.
6. No important AI workflow ships without evaluation coverage and rollback.

---

## 12. The One Question For Every Week

At the end of each week, the team should be able to answer:

**Did we make Casablanca more embedded in real agency workflow, or did we just stay busy?**

If the answer is the second one, the week was not good enough.
