# Casablanca — Financial Model And `EUR1B` Path

**Date:** 2026-04-18  
**Purpose:** define the numbers that make the strategy honest

---

## 1. The Financial Objective

There are two different `EUR1B` outcomes:

### Strategic `EUR1B`

This requires Casablanca to become strategically important enough to command a premium valuation by `2029-04-17`.

### Standalone `EUR1B`

This requires much larger forward revenue and is materially harder inside 36 months.

This model is optimized for the first path while preserving the second as upside.

---

## 2. What The Model Assumes

### Commercial assumptions

- the company sells workflow and seller-acquisition value, not media
- pricing is materially above toy-tool pricing
- active paying agencies are tightly defined
- expansion from buyer response into seller acquisition raises ACV
- partner revenue is meaningful by year 3, but not the core story

### Strategic assumptions

- Spain works first
- Portugal and Italy both matter before month 36
- strategic relevance matters as much as raw ARR
- trust, deployment quality, and benchmark products improve retention and strategic value
- model providers remain replaceable enough that Casablanca keeps workflow ownership

## 4. Revenue Streams

### Stream 1 — Core workflow SaaS

This is the foundation:

- Responde
- inbox
- routing
- reporting
- team controls

### Stream 2 — Seller-acquisition suite

This expands ACV:

- valuation capture
- owner nurture
- performance pages
- listing-presentation assets

### Stream 3 — Onboarding and migration

This should be paid where deployment work is meaningful.

### Stream 4 — Partner revenue

This comes later and should be selective:

- e-sign
- mortgage handoff
- documents
- utilities and moving

### Stream mix target

| Stage | Core workflow SaaS | Seller suite | Onboarding / migration | Partner revenue |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Year 1 exit | 80% | 15% | 5% | 0% |
| Year 2 exit | 68% | 20% | 7% | 5% |
| Year 3 exit | 58% | 20% | 7% | 15% |

The business should still read like software first, with partner monetization increasing strategic depth.

## 5. Pricing Assumptions

### Recommended year-1 packaging

- Solo: `EUR149/month`
- Team: `EUR399/month`
- Growth: `EUR699/month`
- onboarding / migration fees for larger accounts

### Implied active-account economics

The milestone table assumes average recurring revenue per active paying agency climbs over time because:

- weak low-value accounts churn out
- team packages expand
- seller-acquisition modules attach

This is healthier than a low-ARPU, low-usage SMB base.

## 6. Unit Economics Targets

## Year 1 target

- blended recurring revenue per active agency: `EUR250-320/month`
- gross margin: `80-85%`
- CAC per active agency: `EUR800-1,200`
- payback: `<5 months`
- monthly logo churn: `<3.5%`
- weekly active usage in inbox: `75%+`

## Year 2 target

- blended recurring revenue per active agency: `EUR350-420/month`
- gross margin: `78-83%`
- CAC per active agency: `EUR1,200-1,800`
- payback: `<6 months`
- monthly logo churn: `<2.5%`
- seller-suite attach: `30%+`

## Year 3 target

- blended recurring revenue per active agency: `EUR500/month`
- total monthly revenue per active agency including partner layer: `EUR635+`
- gross margin: `75-80%`
- NRR: `120-130%`
- partner revenue meaningful, but not dominant

## 7. Funding Plan

### Pre-seed

- target: `EUR0.75M-1.5M`
- use: 12 months runway, deployment-heavy buildout, founder-led GTM

### Seed

- trigger: `200-500` active paying agencies and strong weekly usage
- target: `EUR4M-6M`
- use: Spain scale, seller-acquisition buildout, onboarding systemization

### Series A

- trigger: `EUR3M-6M` ARR, strong retention, clear path across Spain and into Portugal
- target: `EUR12M-20M`
- use: Portugal and Italy, benchmark data products, partner layer

### Debt

Debt is a tool after PMF, not a substitute for PMF.

## 9. Burn And Runway

### Year 1

- main spend is product, deployment, and founder-led GTM
- keep burn low enough that the company can survive if the wedge takes longer than expected

### Year 2

- burn rises mainly through GTM expansion and customer-facing deployment capacity
- growth should increasingly finance growth if retention is healthy

### Year 3

- the company should be able to choose between aggressive expansion and efficient growth
- if the business still requires blind spending at this stage, the model is weaker than it appears

## 10. The `EUR1B` Math

### Strategic path

By year 3, a plausible `EUR1B` strategic case looks like:

- `EUR42-45M` total annualized revenue run-rate
- clear category ownership in agency revenue workflow
- Spain, Portugal, and Italy all real
- benchmark data and routing power valuable to a strategic buyer

This is the plan we are building toward.

### Standalone path

A standalone `EUR1B` path inside 36 months likely requires:

- more like `EUR65-80M` forward ARR
- stronger partner or fintech attachment
- lower perceived country risk

This is possible only if execution and market pull are exceptional.

---

## 11. Failure Modes

1. too many low-quality logos
2. channel connections remain manual and fragile
3. inbox usage is weak
4. seller-acquisition expansion does not attach
5. partner layer distracts too early
6. new markets open before one-city repeatability
7. pricing remains too low for the ambition
8. evaluation discipline is weak and trust gets damaged

If those failure modes appear, the fix is usually more focus, not more roadmap.

---

## 12. The Single Most Important Financial Number

The single most important number is not total signups.

It is:

**active paying agencies with live channels connected and weekly workflow usage**

That number drives:

- real ARR quality
- strategic value
- expansion probability
- partner attach potential
- the odds that `EUR1B` is more than a slogan
