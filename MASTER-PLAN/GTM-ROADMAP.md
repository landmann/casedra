# Casablanca — Go-To-Market Roadmap

**Date:** 2026-04-18  
**Purpose:** define how we acquire, onboard, expand, and retain agencies fast enough to make the 36-month plan real

---

## 1. GTM Thesis

Casablanca will not win with broad top-of-funnel marketing first.
It will win by:

1. deploying into live workflows
2. proving response improvement quickly
3. turning that proof into case studies and referrals
4. expanding from buyer response into seller acquisition
5. turning repeated proof into a benchmark and reporting ritual agencies depend on

The GTM motion is not:

- demo
- free trial
- hope

The GTM motion is:

- audit
- deploy
- prove
- expand

---

## 2. Ideal Customer Profile

The first ICP is narrow on purpose.

### Primary ICP

- independent or small-chain agencies
- `3–20` agents
- owner or office manager can change process quickly
- meaningful portal spend
- sales-heavy, not rental-heavy
- enough lead volume to feel missed-response pain daily
- concentrated in Madrid

### Secondary ICP

- high-performing solo agents
- network-model agents with enough self-managed volume

### Do not target first

- giant franchises
- low-volume hobby agencies
- teams unwilling to connect live channels
- agencies that only want cheap media tooling

---

## 3. The Core Offer

### Initial promise

**"We help you answer every lead faster, see every conversation in one place, and prove the ROI every week."**

### Expansion promise

**"We help you win more seller instructions by turning your operational performance into a sales asset."**

### Why this matters

This lets Casablanca sell:

- recovered revenue
- team control
- seller acquisition

instead of selling:

- photo generation

## 5. Phase 0 — Design Partner Sprint

**Window:** 2026-04-17 to 2026-05-31

### Objective

Get 10 agencies live, 8 paying, and prove the product works in real traffic.

### Motion

1. founder and realtor team identify design partners
2. run a workflow audit on their current lead handling
3. deploy live channels
4. monitor performance daily
5. secure proof and testimonials

### What the pitch sounds like

Not:

"Look how beautiful the listing looks."

Instead:

"How many leads did you miss last week, and how quickly can we cut that loss?"

### Required assets

- one live walkthrough demo
- one workflow audit template
- one deployment checklist
- one 7-day success report template

### Exit condition

We should know by the end of this phase whether agencies will trust us with live demand.

---

## 6. Phase 1 — Madrid Repeatability

**Window:** 2026-06-01 to 2026-10-31

### Objective

Turn founder-led success into a repeatable local machine.

### Primary motions

1. **Founder-led sales**
   - office visits
   - workflow audits
   - close and deploy

2. **White-glove onboarding**
   - channel connection
   - first inbox setup
   - first weekly report

3. **Case-study loop**
   - saved leads
   - faster response
   - proof for the next account

4. **Referral loop**
   - only after the first agencies are actually seeing value

### Channel priorities

- direct founder outreach
- warm realtor introductions
- office demos
- small local events

### What not to do

- broad paid acquisition
- long content programme as primary engine
- large conferences as a main bet
- enterprise sales

---

## 7. Phase 2 — Multi-City Spain

**Window:** 2026-11-01 to 2027-10-31

### Objective

Replicate the motion in Barcelona and Valencia while deepening Spain.

### What changes

- the pitch expands from response-time improvement to seller-acquisition improvement
- onboarding becomes more systemized
- founders still own strategic accounts, but non-founder operators can run more of the deployment motion

### Commercial priorities

1. Madrid density first
2. Barcelona second
3. Valencia third
4. only then broader Spain

### Core GTM assets by this phase

- workflow audit playbook
- deployment checklist
- weekly ROI report
- seller-acquisition case study
- objection handling library

### The rule

No new city should feel like a brand-new company.
Each new city should look like the same machine with local relationships.

---

## 8. Phase 3 — Portugal And Italy

**Window:** 2027-11-01 to 2029-04-17

### Objective

Prove the company is not a Spain-only special case.

### Sequence

1. Portugal
2. Italy

### How we enter

- hire one local operator with agency credibility
- deploy with design partners
- preserve the same narrow offer
- localize only what is required to get live and sell

### The mistake to avoid

Do not enter a new market with a giant roadmap.
Enter with:

- response workflow
- inbox
- reporting
- then seller acquisition

---

## 9. Onboarding As A Revenue Function

Onboarding is not support.
Onboarding is the bridge between sale and retained revenue.

### Every onboarding must achieve

- at least one live channel connected
- one manager or owner trained
- first weekly report delivered
- first proof point identified
- clear ownership and trust rules for handoff

### The first 200 accounts

The first 200 accounts should receive hands-on onboarding.
That is not inefficient.
That is how we build the company we want.

## 10. Pricing And Packaging In GTM

### Suggested year-1 packages

- **Solo:** `EUR149/month`
- **Team:** `EUR399/month`
- **Growth:** `EUR699/month`
- onboarding or migration fee for larger accounts

### GTM pricing principles

1. sell revenue value, not feature volume
2. do not lead with render counts
3. annual contracts are good if deployment quality is high
4. larger teams should pay for setup and migration

### Recommended order

1. deployment-heavy operator with agency empathy
2. full-stack developer focused on channel and inbox reliability
3. customer success / onboarding lead
4. seller-acquisition GTM operator
5. additional developer for workflow and reporting

- large operations hierarchy

- pipeline review

- churn risk review

### Tuesday to Thursday

- office visits
- demos
- onboarding
- customer reviews

### Friday

- product release
- metrics snapshot
- strategy and prioritization review

This cadence keeps product and GTM fused.

---

## 13. Metrics We Actually Care About

- active paying agencies
- percentage with live channels connected
- inbox weekly active rate
- median first-response time
- percentage of leads answered inside SLA
- activation time from sale to live
- gross churn
- expansion rate into seller-acquisition modules
- deployment time per account
- referral rate

Anything else is secondary until the machine works.

Two added signals matter because they tell us whether we are building a serious AI workflow company:

- trust-breaking incidents
- time from signed account to first believable proof memo

---

## 14. Defensive GTM Plays

### If incumbents copy media features

Ignore it unless it affects workflow adoption.

### If incumbents improve inbox features

Double down on:

- deployment speed
- seller-acquisition expansion
- independent positioning

### If a new AI startup appears

Stay narrow and out-execute.
The real moat is live workflow and benchmark data, not novelty.

### If enterprise chains ask early

Take selected deals only if they do not distort the roadmap.

---

## 15. Category Discipline

Casablanca should market itself like the standard for professional agency operations, not like a generic AI startup.

That means:

- publish proof, not hype
- make weekly reports and benchmarks feel inevitable
- turn the best agencies into examples of the standard
- let the market associate Casablanca with calm, reliable revenue workflow

---

## 16. What The Team Must Not Forget

Casablanca does not become a `EUR1B` company because agencies think it is interesting.

It becomes a `EUR1B` company if agencies rebuild daily revenue behavior around it.

That requires:

- relentless deployment quality
- brutal focus
- weekly usage
- expansion into seller acquisition
- disciplined geographic replication
- a reporting ritual the market starts to expect
