# Casablanca — Read First

**Date:** 2026-04-18  
**Purpose:** this is the shortest possible version of the company plan. Every other document in `MASTER-PLAN` expands this one.

---

## Objective

Build Casablanca into the most important independent revenue workflow layer for real-estate agencies in Spain, then extend that position into Portugal and Italy fast enough to force a `EUR1B` strategic outcome by `2029-04-17`.

This is not the same as saying:

- "we will definitely be a standalone unicorn in 3 years"
- "AI adoption alone will carry us there"
- "if we ship enough features, the market will reward us"

The actual bet is narrower and stronger:

**If Casablanca becomes strategically unavoidable in the three geographies where idealista is strongest, a `EUR1B` outcome becomes plausible.**

---

## One-Sentence Strategy

**Start with a response-time tool, turn it into the agency's live workflow, expand into seller acquisition, then layer a selective marketplace on top of that workflow.**

In shorthand:

`tool -> workflow -> seller acquisition -> marketplace`

---

## What Company We Are Building

Casablanca is:

- an AI-native revenue operating system for agencies
- a WhatsApp-first and inbox-first workflow product
- a seller-acquisition engine once the inbox is trusted
- a selective marketplace only after we control workflow

Casablanca is not:

- an AI media app with delusions of grandeur
- a generic CRM clone
- a consumer portal in disguise
- a back-office ERP
- a brokerage

The way we build this company is also non-negotiable:

- workflow-first
- trust-first
- deployment-heavy
- benchmark-led
- playbook-driven

---

## The Order Of Operations

### 1. Win the first response

`Responde` must help agencies answer every meaningful lead fast, every day, with clear handoff to a human.

### 2. Own the lead stream

The inbox becomes the place where leads arrive, get routed, get tracked, and get measured.

### 3. Win seller instructions

Once Casablanca owns the buyer-side response workflow, it expands into valuation requests, owner nurture, proof-of-performance pages, and listing-presentation generation.

### 4. Add a selective marketplace

Only after the workflow is sticky do we layer in mortgage, e-sign, documents, utilities, and other partner services.

---

## Why This Is The Highest-Confidence Path

This sequence improves our odds because it gives us:

- daily usage instead of occasional usage
- a revenue narrative instead of a content narrative
- higher pricing power
- stronger retention
- benchmark data across agencies
- a more credible strategic-buyer story

Media still matters, but only as:

- a sales demo
- a case-study asset
- a conversion aid inside the workflow

It is not the core business.

---

## The Three Non-Negotiables

1. **Live channels connected**
   No vanity accounts. A paying agency only counts if real channels are live.

2. **Weekly active workflow**
   If the inbox is not used weekly, the account is weak no matter what it pays.

3. **Revenue proof**
   Casablanca must show agencies recovered leads, faster response times, stronger seller capture, or clearer ROI.

---

## The Main Market Bet

Spain is the launch market because:

- it is large
- it is fragmented
- the customer base is operationally messy
- portal dependence is high
- the independent angle is real

But the `EUR1B` plan does not work as a Spain-only story unless we become much more fintech-heavy than we should in the first 24 months.

So the real map is:

- **Spain first**
- **Portugal second**
- **Italy third**

These are not optional expansions. They are part of the thesis.

---

## What Must Be True By Year 3

By `2029-04-17`, Casablanca should look like this:

- `5,500+` active paying agencies
- strong footholds in Spain, Portugal, and Italy
- live control over first response and lead routing
- seller-acquisition workflows deployed and paying for themselves
- network benchmark data across agencies and cities
- category-defining playbooks and weekly operating rituals inside agencies
- a selective partner marketplace generating meaningful revenue
- enough strategic leverage that a large platform, software consolidator, or financial buyer has to care

If by year 3 Casablanca is still mostly:

- a media tool
- a loose bundle of SMB features
- a CRM clone
- or a Spain-only niche SaaS

then the `EUR1B` goal has failed, even if the company is still good.

## Rules That Keep Us Honest

3. No consumer product before the agency workflow is dominant.
4. No broad CRM clone before inbox and seller acquisition are trusted.
5. No heavy marketplace ambition before workflow control exists.
6. No vanity logos in milestone reporting.
7. No soft definitions of success.
