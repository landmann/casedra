# Casablanca — Operating Rhythm

**Date:** 2026-04-18  
**Purpose:** turn the strategy into a repeatable company operating system

---

## 1. The Company Clock

Casablanca runs on four nested clocks:

1. **36-month clock**
   The `EUR1B` strategic-outcome plan.

2. **12-month clock**
   The current year plan and city expansion sequence.

3. **quarter clock**
   The current execution objective.

4. **week clock**
   The real operating unit of the company.

The week clock matters most.
If the week is sloppy, the quarter is fantasy.

## 3. Canonical Definitions

### Active paying agency

An account counts only if:

- it is paying
- at least one live channel is connected
- the inbox is used weekly

### Design partner

A design partner is not just a friendly early user.
A design partner:

- allows live deployment
- shares workflow pain honestly
- joins weekly reviews
- allows Casablanca to measure outcomes

### Live account

An account is live when:

- the primary channel works
- the team can see conversations in the inbox
- takeover works
- first reporting has been delivered

### Healthy account

A healthy account has:

- weekly inbox usage
- stable channel connection
- acceptable response metrics
- at least one visible proof point of value

### At-risk account

An at-risk account has one or more of:

- no weekly inbox usage
- broken or disconnected channels
- confusion about ownership or handoff
- complaints about trust
- no visible proof of value after onboarding

## 5. Weekly Meeting Cadence

### Monday 09:00 — Company Scorecard

**Who:** founders, team leads  
**Duration:** 60 minutes

Agenda:

- gate status
- active paying agencies
- live-channel connection rate
- inbox weekly active rate
- deployment time
- churn and at-risk accounts
- cash and hiring exceptions

Output:

- red / yellow / green status
- top 3 company risks for the week
- decisions that need to happen today

### Monday 10:30 — Product And GTM Priorities

**Who:** founders, product lead, GTM lead, deployment lead  
**Duration:** 60 minutes

Agenda:

- what moved metrics last week
- what blocked deployment
- what feature or process is most important this week
- what gets cut

Output:

- one product priority
- one GTM priority
- one deployment priority

### Tuesday 17:00 — Field Intelligence Review

**Who:** founders, realtor team, deployment lead  
**Duration:** 45 minutes

Agenda:

- top objections heard
- onboarding friction
- sales scripts that worked
- channels or agency behaviors causing trouble

Output:

- updated objection library
- updated deployment checklist
- updated product assumptions if needed

### Wednesday 13:00 — Build And Reliability Review

**Who:** engineering, product, deployment  
**Duration:** 45 minutes

Agenda:

- reliability issues
- parsing failures
- handoff failures
- setup failures
- data quality issues
- evaluation failures
- playbook updates from the field

Output:

- reliability fixes
- instrumentation changes
- deployment safeguards
- evaluation changes
- playbook product changes

### Friday 15:00 — Release And Customer Health Review

**Who:** founders, product, GTM, customer success  
**Duration:** 60 minutes

Agenda:

- what shipped
- who got it first
- which accounts improved
- which accounts are now at risk

Output:

- release note
- at-risk account list
- customer follow-up list for Monday

## 6. Monthly And Quarterly Cadence

### First business day of the month

- financial review
- hiring review
- city readiness review
- pricing review

### End of each month

- compare actuals against gate trajectory
- decide whether the current phase is still valid
- reset internal targets for the next month

### End of each quarter

- keep / cut review of every initiative
- org design review
- capital and runway review
- market expansion readiness review

The quarter review should be brutal.
No initiative should survive on narrative alone.

## 7. Weekly Scorecard

This is the core company scoreboard.

| Metric | Owner | Healthy | Warning | Red |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Active paying agencies vs plan | Founder 1 | on or above plan | <10% behind | >=10% behind |
| Live-channel connection rate | Founder 3 | `>=80%` | `70-79%` | `<70%` |
| Inbox weekly active rate | Founder 3 | `>=75%` | `65-74%` | `<65%` |
| Median first-response time | Founder 2 | improving and inside target | flat | worsening |
| AI workflow eval pass rate | Founder 2 | `>=95%` on core flows | `90-94%` | `<90%` |
| Trust-breaking incidents per month | Founder 2 | `0-1` | `2-3` | `>3` |
| Average deploy time signed -> live | Founder 3 | `<=7 days` | `8-12 days` | `>12 days` |
| Gross logo churn monthly run-rate | Founder 3 | `<3.5%` | `3.5-5%` | `>5%` |
| Seller-suite attach rate once live | Founder 1 | on phase target | below plan | materially below plan |
| Weekly customer proof count | Founder 1 | at or above target | slightly below | weak or zero |
| Weekly proof memo delivery rate | Founder 3 | `>=90%` | `75-89%` | `<75%` |
| Cash runway vs plan | Founder 3 | on plan | tighter than plan | materially below plan |

### Rule

Every Monday, the team should know:

- which metrics are red
- why they are red
- who owns the fix
- whether the fix is product, GTM, or operational

## 8. Decision Rules

Every proposed initiative must answer yes to at least one of these:

1. does it improve activation
2. does it improve weekly usage
3. does it improve retention
4. does it improve ACV
5. does it improve deployment speed
6. does it improve trust or evaluation coverage
7. does it improve strategic relevance in Spain, Portugal, or Italy

If the answer is no, the initiative waits.

### Phase discipline rule

If an initiative belongs to a later phase, it is rejected unless:

- it unblocks the current phase
- or it directly protects the current wedge

---

## 9. Escalation Rules

### If active paying agencies fall behind plan by 2 weeks

- all founders increase field time immediately
- roadmap scope gets cut
- no new speculative work starts

### If live-channel connection rate drops below `70%`

- all non-essential feature work pauses
- deployment quality becomes the top company priority

### If inbox weekly active rate drops below `65%`

- expansion into new modules pauses
- customer interviews and account rescue become immediate priority

### If monthly churn exceeds `5%`

- no city expansion
- no new packaging experiments
- retention fixes take priority

### If trust-breaking incidents exceed `3` in a month

- AI workflow rollouts pause
- evaluation coverage is reviewed immediately
- founder 2 owns the incident recovery plan

### If deployment time goes above `12 days`

- onboarding process is reworked
- deployment tooling gets immediate engineering attention

## 10. Field Operating Rules

### Founder field requirement

Each founder should spend time with real agencies every week.

Minimum:

- 1 field visit per founder per week
- or 2 customer calls if travel is impossible

### Realtor / operator requirement

Every operator should:

- run audits
- document objections
- document setup failures
- document visible customer wins

### Documentation rule

Every customer interaction should update one of:

- objection library
- onboarding checklist
- pricing notes
- roadmap assumptions
- playbook library

If the field does not change the plan, the loop is broken.

---

## 11. Hiring Rules

A hire is justified only if they materially improve one of:

- deployment speed
- weekly usage
- retention
- expansion revenue
- market expansion readiness

### Hiring order rule

Default preference:

1. deployment and onboarding capacity
2. workflow engineering
3. customer success
4. seller-acquisition GTM capacity
5. geographic launch capacity

Brand marketing and organizational layers come later.
