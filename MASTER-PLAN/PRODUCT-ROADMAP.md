# Casablanca — Product Roadmap

**Date:** 2026-04-18  
**Purpose:** define what we build, in what order, and what we explicitly do not build

---

## 1. Product Philosophy

Eight product principles govern the roadmap:

1. **Workflow before features**
   We win by owning the live workflow, not by winning every feature checklist.

2. **Revenue proof before delight**
   Delight matters, but the product must first prove that it improves response, routing, seller capture, or partner conversion.

3. **Deployment is product**
   If agencies cannot connect channels quickly, the product is not good enough.

4. **WhatsApp-first, web-controlled**
   The real channel is messaging. The control layer is the inbox.

5. **Narrow early, deepen later**
   Broad CRM parity too early kills velocity and clarity.

6. **Trust architecture before autonomy**
   Clear ownership, handoff, and escalation matter more than sounding advanced.

7. **Evaluation before rollout**
   Important AI behavior should be measured before it reaches the field.

8. **Playbooks and rituals are product**
   The weekly report, benchmark view, and seller proof pack matter as much as the raw workflow engine.

## 3. Phase 0 — Responde Deployment Layer

**Window:** 2026-04-17 to 2026-05-31  
**Goal:** prove agencies will trust and pay for live response improvement

### Must ship

1. **Live WhatsApp response engine**
   - inbound handling
   - template-safe outbound opening where required
   - controlled first response

2. **Portal lead email bridge**
   - Idealista / Fotocasa / Habitaclia forwarding
   - lead extraction
   - conversation creation

3. **Agent handoff**
   - clear human takeover
   - fast summary
   - no ambiguity about who owns the conversation

4. **Basic inbox**
   - web thread view
   - assignment visibility
   - conversation state

5. **Response reporting**
   - first-response time
   - response coverage
   - handoff tracking

6. **Confidence and escalation controls**
   - explicit low-confidence handoff
   - visible conversation ownership
   - safe fallback behavior

7. **Evaluation harness v1**
   - core response scenarios
   - parsing checks
   - handoff checks

8. **Bundled Media Studio demo**
   - enough to help sales and listing presentations
   - not enough to distract the team into a media-first company

### Must prove

- agencies connect channels
- agencies allow live lead handling
- the handoff earns trust
- agencies will pay above toy-tool pricing

### Explicitly out of scope

- broad CRM objects
- native mobile app depth
- full multi-channel inbox
- complex seller workflows
- large analytics surface
- heavy marketplace work

### Exit criteria

- G0 hit
- first-response improvement visible
- at least 70% of paying agencies connect real channels
- at least 70% of paying agencies use the inbox weekly

## 4. Phase 1 — Inbox And Routing Control

**Window:** 2026-06-01 to 2026-10-31  
**Goal:** make Casablanca the place where agencies work live demand

### Must ship

1. **Unified operational inbox**
   - WhatsApp
   - forwarded portal emails
   - web forms
   - clear ownership and takeover

2. **Routing and assignment**
   - round-robin
   - rule-based routing
   - manager override

3. **Team accountability**
   - SLA views
   - who replied
   - who missed
   - conversation aging

4. **Agency reporting**
   - response-time summaries
   - source quality views
   - owner / manager reports

5. **Migration layer v1**
   - CSV import
   - pragmatic import from incumbent export formats
   - not a perfect migration universe

6. **Click-to-WhatsApp landing pages**
   - simple campaign pages
   - tracking
   - templated agency use cases

7. **Weekly manager proof memo**
   - response-time summary
   - answered-vs-missed view
   - simple benchmark context where available

### Why this phase matters

If we win this phase, Casablanca stops being a helpful tool and starts becoming a workflow dependency.

### Explicitly out of scope

- broad Kanban CRM parity
- full mobile workflow suite
- seller valuation system
- mortgage or document marketplace

### Exit criteria

- G1 and G2 hit
- 75%+ of paying agencies weekly active in inbox
- response-time reporting referenced in sales conversations
- managers rely on Casablanca for team visibility

## 5. Phase 2 — Seller Acquisition OS

**Window:** 2026-11-01 to 2027-04-30  
**Goal:** expand from buyer lead response into instruction-winning workflow

### Must ship

1. **Valuation request flows**
   - embeddable capture
   - basic qualification
   - routing into the inbox and owner workflow

2. **Owner nurture**
   - follow-up sequences
   - AI-assisted replies
   - next-step suggestions

3. **Proof-of-performance pages**
   - response-time proof
   - team performance proof
   - basic social proof and listing outcomes

4. **Listing presentation generator**
   - combine market narrative, photos, seller messaging, and operational proof

5. **Seller pipeline layer**
   - light workflow for owner opportunities
   - no giant CRM rebuild

6. **Account expansion controls**
   - team seats
   - team permissions
   - better manager reporting

7. **Seller playbook templates**
   - follow-up sequences from high-performing accounts
   - instruction-winning presentation patterns
   - reusable owner-response structures

### Why this phase comes before broad CRM depth

Seller acquisition raises ACV and strategic value faster than a broad CRM build.
It also makes Casablanca matter at the exact moment agencies compete for the next listing.

### Explicitly out of scope

- full back-office commission engine
- giant mobile app ambition
- heavy transaction rails
- consumer search product

### Exit criteria

- G3 and G4 hit
- seller-acquisition module attached in at least 30% of active accounts
- clear evidence that Casablanca helps agencies win or accelerate instructions

## 8. Technical Architecture

### Core stack

- Next.js for web surfaces
- Convex for data, realtime, and jobs
- Clerk for identity and organizations
- Stripe for billing
- PostHog for instrumentation
- external messaging providers for channel connectivity

### Product architecture principles

1. **One core data model**
   Conversations, leads, owners, and partner events should not live in disconnected product silos.

2. **Provider abstraction**
   Channel and partner providers must be swappable.

3. **Workflow events first**
   Every meaningful action should become a usable event for reporting, benchmarking, and alerts.

4. **Simple deployment path**
   Internal complexity is acceptable only if agency setup gets easier.

5. **Evaluation layer**
   AI behavior needs testable workflows, not only prompts and optimism.

## 9. AI Strategy

### Year 1

Buy models and optimize the workflow around them.
Keep provider abstraction, confidence controls, and evaluation coverage as first-class priorities.

### Year 2

Tune prompts, evaluations, and benchmark-derived behavior.
Turn field learnings into reusable playbooks across response and seller workflows.

### Year 3

Own the data products and workflow intelligence layer that matter more than raw model ownership.
Expand autonomy only in bounded workflows with clear approvals and measurable quality.

The moat is not a proprietary base model.
The moat is what Casablanca sees, measures, and routes.
Model optionality is strategic.

## 10. What We Will Not Build In The First 36 Months

- consumer search as a core bet
- an iBuyer or brokerage arm
- a giant all-in-one CRM
- a generic app marketplace
- on-premise variants
- broad back-office ERP
- a "freemium forever" motion

Every one of those paths diffuses focus away from the wedge that can actually create a `EUR1B` outcome.

## 12. Release Discipline

1. Weekly releases.
2. Design partners see changes first.
3. Every release must connect to one of:
   - activation
   - weekly usage
   - retention
   - ACV
4. Postmortems for every trust-breaking incident.
5. Any AI workflow release must pass the relevant evaluations and have a rollback path.

This roadmap is only good if the team enforces its order.
